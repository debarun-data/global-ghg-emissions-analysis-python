# 🌍 Global GHG Emissions Analysis (1950–2024)

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes 70+ years of global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions to uncover trends, inequalities, and key contributors to climate change.

Using Python-based data analysis, the study explores how emissions have evolved over time and how responsibility is distributed across countries.

---

## ❓ Problem Statement
How have global greenhouse gas emissions evolved from 1950 to 2024, and what do patterns of growth, inequality, and concentration reveal about climate responsibility?

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze long-term global emission trends
- Identify top emitting countries
- Compare per capita vs total emissions
- Understand inequality in emission distribution

---

## 📊 Key Analysis Performed
- 📈 Global emission trend (1950–2024)
- 🏆 Top 10 emitting countries
- 🌍 Per capita emission comparison
- 🔄 Country-level trend comparison (India vs China)
- 📊 Distribution analysis (skewness of emissions)
- 🔗 Correlation between total and per capita emissions

---

## 💡 Key Insights
- Global emissions have **more than tripled since 1950**
- A small number of countries dominate total emissions
- Per capita emissions reveal **hidden inequality**
- Emission distribution is **highly skewed**
- Developing economies are showing **rapid growth trends**

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaways
- Climate change is not evenly caused — it is **highly concentrated**
- Total emissions show **impact**, per capita shows **responsibility**
- Data clearly indicates the need for **targeted global action**
